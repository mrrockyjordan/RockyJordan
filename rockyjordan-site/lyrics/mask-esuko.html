About This Song

"Mask Esuko" (meaning "Wear the Mask") is Rocky Jordan's Telugu rap response to the COVID-19 pandemic. Part public service announcement, part social satire, the track tells the stories of real people — Shiva who refused to wear a mask and ended up in Apollo hospital, Saleem bhai who thought he was too strong to get sick. The spoken-word bridge is one of Rocky's most powerful moments — describing mothers losing children, fathers carrying sons to cremation grounds, and hospitals with no beds. It turns a public health message into a gut punch of storytelling.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Mask Esuko mean?
Mask Esuko means Wear the Mask in Telugu. The song is a COVID-era public health message wrapped in rap storytelling.
What is the spoken word section about?
Rocky describes real stories of pandemic suffering — mothers losing children, fathers carrying bodies, hospitals with no beds, and people dying because they couldn’t pay bills.
